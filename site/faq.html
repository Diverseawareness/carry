About Carry

What is Carry?
Carry is a live skins game tracker for golf. Create a skins game, invite players via SMS, and track every skin, carry, and payout in real time from your phone. Scores sync across all groups every 15 seconds, push notifications alert you when someone wins a skin, and everything works offline on the course. Settle up instantly with Venmo deep links.
What is a skins game?
A skins game is a golf betting format where each hole has a value (a "skin"). The player with the lowest score on a hole wins that skin. If two or more players tie, the skin can either carry over to the next hole or be voided, depending on your group's rules.
Who is Carry for?
Anyone who plays skins. Whether you're in a weekly club game with 16 players across four tee times, or a casual weekend round with three friends, Carry handles the math and keeps everyone honest.
What devices does Carry support?
Carry is available on iPhone running iOS 17 or later.

Pricing

Is Carry free?
Carry offers a free tier that lets you create 1 skins game group, view your 3 most recent rounds, and access the last round leaderboard. Carry Premium ($29.99/year or $4.99/month) unlocks unlimited groups, full round history, all-time season leaderboard, and season statistics.
Is there a free trial?
Yes. New users can try Carry Premium free for 7 days. No charge until the trial ends, and you can cancel anytime.
How do I cancel my subscription?
Subscriptions are managed through the App Store. On your iPhone, go to Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions to manage or cancel Carry Premium. You'll keep access until the end of your billing period.
How many Quick Games can I play for free?
Free users get 3 Quick Games per calendar month. Premium subscribers get unlimited Quick Games.
What happens after I use my 3 free Quick Games?
You'll be prompted to start a 7-day free trial of Carry Premium, which includes unlimited Quick Games and group features.

How Skins Work

What are carries?
When carries are enabled and a hole is tied (no outright winner), the skin's value carries over and adds to the next hole. This means a single hole can be worth multiple skins. For example, if holes 5 and 6 are tied, hole 7's skin is worth 3x. You can toggle carries on or off in your game settings.
How are payouts calculated?
The total pot (number of players × buy-in) is divided by the number of skins won. Each skin is worth an equal share. For example: 4 players at $50 buy-in = $200 pot. If 10 skins are won across 18 holes, each skin is worth $20. A player who won 3 skins takes home $60.
Can I use handicaps?
Yes. Set each player's handicap index and choose a percentage (100%, 80%, etc.) in game settings. Carry automatically calculates playing handicaps based on the course's slope and rating, and applies strokes to the right holes.
How many players can be in a game?
There's no hard limit. Carry automatically groups players into foursomes and lets you stagger tee times at 8, 10, or 12 minute intervals. Whether it's 4 players or 16, everything is tracked in one game.

Payments & Settling Up

Does Carry handle money?
No. Carry calculates exact payouts and connects you to Venmo for the transfer. No money flows through the app. You can also settle up in cash or any other way you prefer.
Do I need Venmo?
Venmo integration is optional. It's a convenience feature that pre-fills the amount and recipient. You're free to settle up however your group prefers.

How do I sign up?

Is my data secure?
Yes. Carry uses industry-standard encryption and secure cloud infrastructure. Your scores and game data are only visible to members of your skins groups. We never sell your data. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Setting Up a Game

How do I create a skins game?
Go to the Skins tab and tap +. Name your game, set the buy-in amount, search for and select your course, then invite players. You'll be added automatically as the first member and scorer.
How do I invite players?
From your skins group, tap "Invite & Manage." Send SMS invitations directly from the app using the native message composer, or search for existing Carry users by name. Invited players appear as pending until they accept.
How do tee times work with multiple groups?
When you have more than 4 players, Carry auto-groups them into foursomes. Set a base tee time, then use Consecutive Tee Times (+8, +10, or +12 min intervals) to stagger each group. All tee times must be set before you can start the round.
Can I add players after creating the game?
Yes. Open your skins group and tap "Invite & Manage" to add new members at any time. New players will appear in the tee time groups as pending until they accept the invite.
How do I set up carries?
In your skins group settings (tap the gear icon), toggle "Carries" on or off. When on, tied holes carry their skin value to the next hole. When off, tied holes are dead skins.
What is a Quick Game?
A Quick Game is a streamlined way to start a skins game in seconds without creating a group or sending invites. Only one Carry account is needed per group — the scorer types in player names, picks a course, and starts scoring right away.
How do I start a Quick Game?
Tap the + button on the Games tab, select Quick Game, fill in player names and handicaps, choose a course, and tap Continue.

Scoring

How do I start scoring?
The game creator starts the round from the skins group. Once started, all members see a "LIVE Score Card" button on their Home and Skins tabs. The designated scorer in each group enters scores hole by hole.
Who is the scorer?
The first confirmed player in each tee time group is the default scorer. You can change the scorer by tapping the "Scorer" badge in the tee time card and selecting a different player.
Can I edit scores?
Yes, at any time. Tap any score cell on the scorecard to change it. Skins calculations update automatically. After the round, the creator can still edit scores from the "Edit Scores" button on the results screen.
What do the colors on the scorecard mean?
Circle (green fill) = birdie or better. No border = par. Square outline = bogey or worse. In the skins row, a gold avatar means that player won the skin. A pulsating avatar shows the current leader on an unsettled hole. A pulsating gold dot means no scores have been entered for that hole yet. Skins update live as scores come in from all groups.
Do I need cell service to score?
No. Scores save to your device instantly and sync to the cloud when you have a connection. You won't lose any data playing in a dead zone. When connectivity returns, all pending scores upload automatically.
Do Quick Game players need a Carry account?
No. Only one person per group needs a Carry account — they're the scorer. Everyone else is entered by name and handicap. No sign-ups, no downloads required to play.
Can I turn a Quick Game into a recurring group?
Yes. After the round, you'll be asked if you want to play again. Tap "Yes" and your Quick Game converts into a full skins group. You can name it, set up weekly tee times, and invite players to join Carry so they can track their own stats.
What's the difference between a Quick Game and a Skins Group?
A Quick Game is a one-off — one scorer, no invites, start in seconds. A Skins Group is a recurring setup where all players have accounts, can score independently, and track their stats over time. You can always convert a Quick Game into a group after the round.

Results & History

Where do I see results?
Active games show a live card on your Home tab. When all groups finish, tap "Show Final Results" to see the full breakdown: who won which skins and the total payout per player.
What are pending results?
When some groups have finished but others are still playing, results are "pending." You can see which skins are decided and which are still being contested. Final results appear once all groups complete 18 holes.
How do I settle up?
The final results screen shows exactly who owes what. Use your preferred payment app to settle up based on the amounts shown.
Where is my round history?
Past rounds appear in the "Recent Games" section on your Home tab. Free users see their 3 most recent rounds. Carry Premium ($29.99/year or $4.99/month) unlocks full round history. Each group's leaderboard has two tabs: "Last Round" shows the most recent round, and "All Time" (Premium) shows cumulative skins won, total winnings, and season statistics across all rounds.
Can I share results?
Yes. On the final results screen, tap the share icon (top right) to share a text summary of the round results with your group via Messages, email, or any other app.

Notifications & Offline

What notifications does Carry send?
Carry uses Apple Push Notification service (APNs) to send real-time alerts, including game-started notifications when the creator starts a round, group invite notifications, round completion updates, and scorer assignment changes. You can also receive tee time reminders and cross-group skin-won notifications. You can manage notification preferences at any time through your iPhone Settings.
Does Carry work offline on the course?
Yes. Scores save to your device instantly and skins calculations happen locally. If you lose cell signal during a round, everything keeps working. When connectivity returns, pending scores automatically sync to the cloud and cross-group score syncing resumes.
Can I watch a game without scoring?
Yes. Viewers see the same live scorecard, skins results, and celebrations as scorers, but cannot enter or edit scores. This is great for spectators or friends following along remotely.

Profile & Settings

How do I update my profile?
Go to the Profile tab. Tap your name or photo to open the edit sheet. You can change your display name, avatar photo (take a new one with your camera or pick from your photo library), handicap index, home club, club membership status, and GHIN number.
What is home club vs. club membership?
Home Club is the golf course you designate as your home course. Club Membership indicates whether you're a member or non-member. Both are optional and visible to other players in your groups.
What is the GHIN number?
GHIN is the Golf Handicap and Information Network, managed by the USGA. You can enter your GHIN number in your profile for reference. Automatic handicap index syncing via GHIN is coming soon — for now, you can manually set your handicap index in your profile settings.
How do I update my handicap?
On the Profile tab, tap your handicap index to open the picker. Select your current index. This is used to calculate playing handicaps for skins games that use handicap-adjusted scoring.
